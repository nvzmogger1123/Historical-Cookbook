Tagine (Morocco)

History: Tagine is a slow-cooked stew, traditionally made in a clay pot. It reflects Moroccan flavors with spices like cumin, coriander, and cinnamon.

Ingredients: Lamb or chicken, onions, tomatoes, preserved lemon, olives, cumin, and saffron.

Preparation: Meat is slow-cooked with spices and vegetables until tender.

Waffles (Belgium)

History: Belgian waffles originated in the Middle Ages and became globally famous for their fluffy texture and deep pockets.

Ingredients: Flour, eggs, milk, sugar, yeast, butter.

Preparation: The batter is prepared and cooked in a waffle iron until golden brown.

Adobo (Philippines)

History: Adobo dates back to pre-colonial times when Filipinos preserved meat using vinegar and salt.

Ingredients: Chicken or pork, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, black pepper.

Preparation: The meat is marinated and then slow-cooked until tender.
